Human Resource Management in Tourism and Hospitality

In this course, learners will explore contemporary issues in managing human capital in the hotel and tourism industry, from macro- and micro-perspectives, and learn how to tackle them.

Course Duration: 45 hours

Learning Objectives

At the end of the course, the learner will be able to:

  • Evaluate and discuss theories of managing human resources in the hotel and tourism industry.
  • Appraise and analyze human resource functions and employee motivation.
  • Evaluate factors affecting organizational culture development.
  • Assess the relationship between the hotel and tourism industry and their society.
  • Analyze HR research journals for applicable methodologies.
  • Identify and solve human resources problems in the hotel and tourism context.

Course Outline

  • Basics & Theories of HRM
  • Human Capital of the Hospitality Industry
  • Basics of Organizational Culture
  • Understanding HR Problems in the Hospitality Industry

Course Benefit

This program awards a Human Resource Management in Hospitality & Tourism Completion Certificate from the Academy. Successful completion grants certification from KHDA, and students can progress to OTHM courses related to Tourism & Hospitality Management.

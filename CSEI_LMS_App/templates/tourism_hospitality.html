Tourism & Hospitality Management

The course provides the essentials of Tourism & Hospitality Management, covering various aspects and subjects within the field. It gives learners comprehensive knowledge about the various segments of the hospitality industry.

Learning Objectives

At the end of the course, the learner will be able to:

  • Understand the basics of housekeeping, front office management, and hotel management.
  • Become acquainted with tourism management and tourism marketing.
  • Demonstrate and understand the segments of hospitality management.

Course Outline

  • Introduction to Housekeeping
  • Introduction to Front Office
  • Introduction to Hotel Management
  • Introduction to Tourism Management
  • The Tourism System, Tourism Product, Tourist Markets
  • Economic and Socio-Cultural Impacts of Tourism
  • Basics of Hospitality Management
  • Destination Management, NTO
  • Travel Geography and its Influence on Air Transport
  • Sources of Information for Fare Construction Principles
  • Travel Agents and Agencies
  • Starting a Travel Agency
  • Services Offered by a Travel Agency
  • Tour Operation and Costing of Tours
  • Reservations and Documentation
  • The Impact of the Internet on the Tourism and Hospitality Industry
  • Application of the Internet for the Tourism Sector
  • Tourism Enterprises and Entrepreneurship
  • Mechanized Reservation Systems
  • Airline Packages and Pricing Travel Itineraries Online
  • Hotel and Tour Operators’ Sites Online

Course Benefit

This program awards a Tourism & Hospitality Management Completion Certificate from the Academy. Upon successful completion of 30 hours, learners will receive a certification from KHDA. After completion, students can also join OTHM courses related to their field as part of their progression.

Required Documents

  • Higher Academic Certificates
  • Passport Copy
  • Photograph
  • Local Identity (Citizenship)
  • Experience Letter (Optional)

Requirements

  • Evidence of numeracy and literacy skills through the last studied high school report.
  • Work experience demonstrated through an updated resume (for professionals).
  • Additional English language proficiency may apply if the medium of teaching is not in English.

Study Mode

  • Full-Time / Part-Time: Weekend Based
  • Location: On Campus / Online

Career Outcomes

  • This qualification provides a pathway to start working in any sector of the hospitality industry. Employers include restaurants, hotels, motels, catering operations, clubs, pubs, cafés, and coffee shops.
